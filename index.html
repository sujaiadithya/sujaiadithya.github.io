Laptop Market Analysis using SQL

Check out this project in GitHub

In this project, I conducted a comprehensive analysis of laptop specifications using advanced SQL queries. The focus was on deriving actionable insights from a dataset containing detailed information about laptops, including CPU benchmarks, GPU classifications, screen resolutions, storage capacities, and pricing. The analysis involved ranking top-performing laptops, evaluating distribution patterns across various USB types, and comparing screen sizes across GPU classes. Additionally, I identified key patterns in laptop resolutions related to RAM capacity and storage size. A specific emphasis was placed on understanding the pricing dynamics in the refurbished laptop market, particularly in relation to storage capacities. The project showcases my proficiency in SQL by tackling complex queries that involve ranking, percentage distributions, and average calculations, making it a valuable addition to my portfolio for demonstrating expertise in data-driven decision-making.

Data-Driven Road Safety Enhancements: A Vision Zero Case Study in San Francisco

Check out this project in Tableau Public

In this project, I contributed to San Francisco's Vision Zero initiative, which aims to eliminate traffic fatalities and severe injuries by 2024. Utilizing a data-driven approach, I identified high-risk locations, collision patterns, and contributing factors through predictive modeling and advanced clustering techniques like ARIMA and DBSCAN. The project involved analyzing trends in severe and fatal collisions, forecasting future high-risk areas, and developing targeted strategies to enhance road safety. These strategies included improving public transportation quality, increasing fines for traffic violations, and expanding red light camera programs. The project's success criteria focused on creating a safer environment for all road users through sustainable and scalable solutions, demonstrating my ability to apply data science to real-world urban safety challenges.

Analyzing Performance Trends in Formula 1 Racing

In this project, I conducted an in-depth exploratory visualization analysis of Formula 1 racing performance trends using data sourced from Kaggle. By integrating 11 distinct datasets in Tableau, I uncovered key insights into the dominance of various teams over the years, the correlation between qualifying positions and final race outcomes, and the impact of a driver's age on performance. Additionally, I explored the concept of home advantage in Formula 1, concluding that unlike other sports, there isn’t a significant home advantage for drivers. Throughout the analysis, I tackled challenges such as cleaning and standardizing data, handling missing values, and managing complex data relationships, ultimately creating a comprehensive and visually compelling narrative of the sport’s competitive dynamics.
